About Us

ForsythCTF was started to help students in middle school to early high school learn more about the growing field of cybersecurity. We found that not many schools give much instruction about this topic, and it will be especially important in coming years. As students who have had the oppurtunity to learn about cybersecurity, we wanted to spread this knowledge to better our futures.

  • Participants will learn through the Capture the Flag style, where they are given multiple challenges and have to solve them.
  • The CTF covers topics such as the CIA triad, cryptography, forensics, basic coding, and more.
  • The cybersecurity market is predicted to grow by around 8% by the end of this decade.

Participants said that it was a fun way to learn some of the basics and that they are interested to learn more. Our goal is to conduct as many events as possible to ensure maximum outreach.

The Process

  • Creating Challenges

    The most important part of making a CTF benefical to the participants are the challenges themselves. We ensure that our challenges are not only engaging, but also educational, in the hopes that competitors pursue this field in the future.

  • Hosting

    To ensure that our competitions run flawlessly, we have chosen both Google Cloud Platform and DigitalOcean to host our challenges, so that we can use the most optimal hardware configuration.

  • Frameworks

    To make our competitions visually appealing to the participants, we have chosen to go with CTFd as our framework. This provides many impressive capabilities such as a live leaderboard and advanced player statistics.

  • Venues

    The final step in the process of creating a CTF is the venue. We have hosted competitions both virtually for students attending high school/college, and in-person at local libraries for students attending middle school.
